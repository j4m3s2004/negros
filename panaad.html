PANAAD FESTIVAL

Panaad Festival — The Festival of Festivals

The Panaad Festival is one of the largest celebrations in Negros Occidental, held annually in Bacolod City. Known as the “Festival of Festivals,” it brings together different cities and municipalities to showcase their culture and traditions.

It is a grand event that highlights the unity and diversity of the entire province.

A Showcase of Local Culture

Each town in Negros Occidental sets up its own pavilion, displaying local products, traditions, and unique identity. Visitors can explore these areas and experience the culture of different places all in one location.

This makes Panaad a great way to learn about the province without traveling far.

Food, Products, and Exhibits

The festival also features a wide variety of local food, handicrafts, and products. Visitors can try regional delicacies and buy locally made goods that represent the province’s industries and creativity.

It is both a cultural and economic showcase of Negros Occidental.

Performances and Competitions

Panaad includes performances, cultural shows, and competitions between different towns. These events highlight traditional dances, music, and talents from across the province.

The lively atmosphere makes it an exciting experience for visitors.

Why Experience Panaad Festival?

Panaad Festival offers a complete cultural experience in one place. It allows visitors to see the traditions, food, and lifestyle of the entire province in a single event.

It is a meaningful celebration that reflects the pride and identity of Negros Occidental.
